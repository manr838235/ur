Last Updated: May 2026

Welcome to Upcoin Wallet. Your privacy and the security of your digital assets are our highest priorities. This page outlines how we handle your data, our legal obligations, and the risks associated with cryptocurrency services.

1. Information We Collect

We collect only the necessary data to provide our services and ensure the security of your account:

  • Identity Data: Full name, email address, date of birth, and official government-issued identification documents (e.g., passport or ID card) for account verification.
  • Blockchain Data: Wallet addresses, transaction hashes, transfer amounts, and timestamps associated with blockchain activities.
  • Technical Data: IP addresses, device types, operating systems, and browser cookies used to improve platform performance and prevent fraudulent access.

2. Legal Compliance & Fraud Prevention (KYC/AML)

  • Identity Verification: Our platform strictly complies with international Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations to combat financial crimes.
  • Data Sharing: We do not sell or rent your personal data to third-party advertisers. However, we are legally required to share your transaction details and identity information with regulatory or judicial authorities upon receiving official legal requests.
  • Security Partners: We may cooperate with third-party blockchain intelligence and cybersecurity firms to analyze transactions, detect malicious patterns, and prevent fraudulent operations.

3. Asset Security & Protection

  • Encryption: All personal data and platform communications are fully encrypted using industry-standard secure protocols (SSL/TLS).
  • Fund Protection: To ensure maximum safety against potential online breaches, the vast majority of digital assets are stored securely in offline Cold Storage systems.
  • User Responsibility: While we provide advanced security tools like Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), securing your password, API keys, and 2FA recovery codes remains your exclusive personal responsibility.

4. Risk Disclosure & Disclaimer

  • Market Volatility: Cryptocurrencies are highly volatile and speculative assets involving extreme price fluctuations. The platform is not responsible for any financial losses resulting from your trading or investment decisions.
  • Irreversibility of Blockchain: Transactions executed on blockchain networks are permanent and final. Once sent, they cannot be reversed. You are entirely responsible for verifying the accuracy of the recipient's wallet address before authorizing any transfer. The platform assumes no liability for assets sent to incorrect addresses.
